Who I am

More than just
a student.

I chose to pursue a diploma in Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics because I believe everyone should feel safe when using technology and digital devices. Growing up, I was always the kind of person who wanted to understand how things worked, whether it was taking apart gadgets, figuring out why systems behaved a certain way, or simply asking one too many questions.

That curiosity eventually led me to cybersecurity, a field where asking questions and thinking critically are not just encouraged but essential. Since then, I have channelled that same curiosity into my studies, projects, and the way I approach new challenges. My goal is to use my skills and knowledge to help create a safer cyber environment in the future.

Apart from my studies, I enjoy living an active and healthy lifestyle. I love playing sports, especially football, and I even have my own football team where we occasionally play against other teams. I also enjoy meeting new people and spending time with my friends because I believe maintaining strong friendships is important.

During my free time, I participate in volunteering activities, mainly at the mosque, where I help the staff organise events and community activities. I also enjoy giving back to the community by taking part in workshops, such as helping elderly people learn how to use digital devices.

"I believe every person you meet and every experience you go through teaches you something, if you are paying attention."

I carry that belief into everything I do. I try to approach life with openness, patience, and a genuine desire to keep improving, not just as a professional, but as a person.
